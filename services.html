What is a DBQ?

A Disability Benefits Questionnaire (DBQ) is a standardized medical form used by the Department of Veterans Affairs to evaluate your disability claim. It captures specific, condition-related information that the VA needs to determine your disability rating.

Think of it as a detailed medical report that speaks the VA's language. When filled out by a qualified, licensed professional, a DBQ provides the VA with the exact information they need to process your claim — no more, no less.

While the VA can conduct its own evaluations through Compensation & Pension (C&P) exams, you also have the right to submit a DBQ completed by a private provider. This gives you more control over your evaluation experience and timeline.
