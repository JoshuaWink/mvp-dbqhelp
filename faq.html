Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about DBQ evaluations and the VA claims process

FAQ List

What is a DBQ (Disability Benefits Questionnaire)?

A DBQ is a standardized medical form used by the VA to evaluate disability claims. It captures specific information about your condition that the VA needs to determine your disability rating. A licensed medical professional completes the form based on their evaluation of your condition.

Who can complete a DBQ?

A DBQ can be completed by any licensed medical professional qualified to evaluate your condition. For mental health DBQs, this means a licensed medical examiner or psychiatrist. At DBQ Help, all evaluations are conducted by licensed medical examiners.

What is a nexus letter?

A nexus letter is a professional medical opinion that establishes a connection between your military service and your current medical condition. It provides the VA with evidence that your condition is "at least as likely as not" related to your service. This is often a critical piece of evidence in supporting your disability claim.

How long does a DBQ evaluation take?

A typical mental health DBQ evaluation takes approximately 1–2 hours, depending on the complexity of your condition. We also spend additional time reviewing your records before the evaluation to ensure thoroughness.

Do I need a referral to get a private DBQ evaluation?

No referral is needed. Veterans have the right to obtain a private DBQ evaluation from a qualified provider. Simply contact us to schedule your evaluation — call 916-202-0558 or use our contact form.

What conditions do you evaluate?

We specialize in providing medical evaluations for both mental and physical health conditions. Our expertise is in PTSD increases, depression, anxiety, orthopedic conditions, neurological conditions, and others! If you're unsure whether your condition qualifies, give us a call and we'll be happy to discuss your situation.

How much does a DBQ evaluation cost?

Our evaluation fees vary based on the complexity of the case and services needed. We're happy to discuss pricing during your initial consultation call. We believe in transparent pricing with no hidden fees.

Do you serve veterans throughout California?

Yes! We serve veterans throughout California, including Sacramento, Los Angeles, San Diego, San Francisco, and everywhere in between. Contact us to schedule your evaluation.
