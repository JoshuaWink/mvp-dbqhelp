## What They're Doing Right (That We Should Learn From)

### 1. City-Specific Landing Pages
**Vet Resource Group** has individual pages for every major CA city:
- `/sacramento-ca-nexus-letters/`
- `/los-angeles-ca-nexus-letters/`
- `/san-diego-ca-nexus-letters/`

**Action**: Create city landing pages targeting Sacramento, LA, San Diego, San Francisco, San Jose.

### 2. Doctor's Face + Name Front and Center
**VetNexusLetter** leads with "Dr. Deborah Miller" — name, photo, credentials, direct engagement.
**Sanova** uses testimonials with real quotes.

**Action**: This aligns perfectly with Dr. Green's request. His photo + personal message is a competitive advantage.

### 3. Testimonials / Social Proof
Multiple competitors use veteran testimonials with specific outcomes:
- "My appeal was approved within weeks"
- "VA approved my claim based on your exam and nexus letter"

**Action**: Get permission from past clients for testimonials. Even 2-3 would be impactful.

### 4. FAQ + Educational Content
**California Psychological** has extensive FAQ covering:
- Difference between Nexus Letter and IMO
- Why VA doctors can't write nexus letters
- What DBQ forms cover

**Action**: Our FAQ is solid but could expand with California-specific Q&As.

### 5. Speed / Turnaround Messaging
Competitors emphasize fast turnaround:
- "Fast, Accurate, Veteran-Focused"
- "quickly and often at a much lower cost"

**Action**: If DBQ Help has fast turnaround, call it out explicitly.
